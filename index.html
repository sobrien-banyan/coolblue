SEER April 1, 2019

Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio (SEER) is an estimation of the average efficiency of an air conditioner throughout the entire cooling season and is used to describe the efficiency of refrigeration and air conditioning systems. The SEER rating is the estimated cooling done by the unit during its normal usage divided by the estimated electric energy input in watt-hours during this time. As a general rule of thumb the higher the SEER rating the more efficient and higher the savings.

The government has mandated improved energy efficiency measures. All new air conditioners must achieve a SEER rating of 13 of higher. Older units may have a SEER rating a low as 6. To qualify for an Energy Star rating, air conditioners must have a SEER rating of 15 or higher. Variable speed compressors, variable speed blower motors, better refrigerants and increased use of automatic control systems have allowed systems to achieve SEER ratings higher than 20. As a unit gets older it will lose its efficiency. There are simple steps that can be taken to maintain a units SEER. Regular check ups can be useful in spotting problems. Left untreated a small issue can get worse. Keeping the air flow around an outdoor unit, cleaning coils and changing the air filter on a regular bases can all help with keeping the system running in peak condition.

Central Air Conditioning Most split air conditioning systems have their components in two or more separate locations. A split system’s condensing unit is often located outdoors, and its evaporating unit is places indoors. The evaporator is installed in a central air handler cabinet that is connected to network of ducts. The duct system moves the air to and from the air handler. The air filters can be located any where in the return duct network, it is usually found at a return register or at the inlet of the air handler. Thermostats August 1, 2019

There are so many types of thermostat that control cooling and heating systems. Cooling thermostat and heating thermostat are the two basic types and when combined its called a combination thermostat or a heating-cooling thermostat. Some combination thermostats will automatically change from heating to cooling.

Modern thermostats can be much more responsive and intuitive. Wi-Fi enabled thermostats can be operated from anywhere in the world using a smartphone. Learning thermostats can remember an occupant’s desired set point and adapt the HVAC system operation to automatically program On and Off cycles and temperature set points. This can significantly reduce the energy used over the units life.

Programmable thermostats. The correct thermostat can save the home owner money. We recommend a programmable thermostat. Programmable thermostats have a micro processor that functions as a clock and allows users to select different indoor climate conditions for different blocks of time. The programming can maximize the efficiency of a system. An energy saving tip is to change the temperature of the system at the thermostat to decrease the run time during the night and when not at home.

Older thermostat used mercury as a switching element. Mercury is a very toxic substance and prolonged exposure may cause severe health effects. The use of mercury thermostats is being phased out and banned in some nation. If you have an older thermostat that has mercury contact us or book an appointment so we can safely replace it for a newer one.
